# Background on Paul Graham

Name: Paul Graham
Primary Role: Entrepreneur, Venture Capitalist, Essayist
Primary Language: English
Industry / Domain: Technology, Startups, Venture Capital
Notable For: Co-founding Y Combinator; essays on startups and programming

- Co-founder of **Viaweb** (later acquired by Yahoo and renamed Yahoo Store), one of the first web-based applications.
- Co-founder of **Y Combinator**, a startup accelerator that funded companies such as Airbnb, Dropbox, and Stripe.
- Author of influential essays on startups, programming languages, wealth, and innovation, widely read by founders and engineers.
- Creator of the programming language **Arc**, and early advocate of Lisp.
- Author of books including _Hackers & Painters_ (2004), a collection of essays on software and startups.

Other notes about his blog/writing style:

- Publishes long-form essays on paulgraham.com, typically focused on startups, technology, and societal trends.
- Essays are frequently cited within the startup ecosystem and among technology founders.
- Writes independently rather than through a corporate publication.
